How often should a commercial kitchen clean its grease trap?

Most kitchens benefit from cleaning every one to three months depending on volume. High-output kitchens may need monthly service. We can set a recurring schedule that keeps you compliant and prevents backups.

Is hydro jetting safe for older pipes?

For most pipes, yes. We perform a camera inspection first to confirm the line can handle high pressure. If we find significant corrosion or damage, we advise against jetting and explain your repair options instead.

How is hydro jetting different from snaking?

Snaking punches a hole through a clog. Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water to scour the full pipe wall clean, removing grease, roots, and scale, so the line flows freely and stays clear longer.

What is a sewer scope inspection and when do I need one?

A sewer scope sends a high-definition camera through your sewer line to find cracks, root intrusion, bellies, grease, and blockages. It is useful before buying a home, when troubleshooting recurring backups, or for preventive maintenance.

Should I get a sewer scope before buying a home?

Yes. Many sewer defects are hidden underground and are not covered by a standard home inspection. A sewer scope can reveal issues that cost thousands to repair, giving you peace of mind and negotiating leverage before closing.

How do you find a hidden water leak without digging?

We use non-invasive leak detection such as acoustic equipment, pressure testing, thermal imaging, and utility locating to pinpoint hidden leaks behind walls, under slabs, and underground, so any repair is targeted rather than exploratory.

What are the signs of a damaged sewer line?

Common signs include slow or recurring drain backups, sewage odors, gurgling fixtures, multiple drains backing up at once, and wet or sunken spots in the yard. A camera inspection confirms the cause before repair.
