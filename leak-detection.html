Leak Detection in Denver, CO

Find hidden leaks without tearing up your property — non-invasive detection technology that saves time, money, and disruption.

A hidden water or sewer leak can waste thousands of gallons, drive up utility bills significantly, undermine foundations, and cause structural damage — all without obvious visible signs until the problem is already serious. Traditional leak finding used to mean digging or opening walls to locate the source. Modern technology has changed that entirely.

Jet Force uses professional leak detection equipment — including acoustic listening devices, pressure testing, thermal imaging, and ground microphones — to pinpoint leaks in underground water lines, sewer laterals, irrigation systems, and in-slab plumbing without unnecessary excavation. We locate the problem precisely so any repair work is targeted, not exploratory.

How Leak Detection Works

We begin by assessing your symptoms: unexplained water bill increases, wet spots, reduced pressure, or known pipe age and history. Based on that information, we isolate the likely section and deploy the appropriate detection method for the situation.

Acoustic listening equipment detects the sound signature of escaping pressurized water through soil and concrete. Pressure differential testing confirms leaks in closed systems. Thermal imaging identifies temperature anomalies indicating water movement in walls or slabs. Once the leak is located, we mark the precise spot and provide a written report that includes location, estimated depth, and our recommended repair approach.

Signs You Need This Service

  • Unexplained spike in the water bill with no change in usage
  • Visible wet spots or persistently soggy patches in the yard with no rain
  • Low water pressure throughout the property with no known cause
  • Sound of running water when all fixtures and appliances are off
  • Warm or discolored spots appearing on slab floors
  • Foundation cracks or soil settling near a water or sewer line

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is non-invasive leak detection?
When used by experienced technicians on appropriate pipe materials, acoustic and pressure-based detection methods can locate leaks within one to two feet in most conditions. Results are affected by pipe material, depth, and soil type. We will give you an honest assessment of confidence level before any repair recommendation is made.
Can you detect sewer leaks as well as water line leaks?
Yes. For sewer leaks, we use smoke testing and camera inspection in addition to acoustic methods. Sewer leaks are particularly important to detect early — they cause soil contamination, accelerate root intrusion, and can lead to significant structural damage if left unresolved.
What happens after the leak is found?
We provide a full written location report with all findings. If the repair falls within our scope — underground pipe repair, sewer lateral work, or service line replacement — we can handle it directly. If it is a specialty situation, the report gives any contractor exactly what they need to begin work efficiently and without guesswork.
