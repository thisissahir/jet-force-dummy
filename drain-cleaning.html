How Drain Cleaning Works

Our technician arrives, assesses the affected drain, and determines the best approach based on the type of drain, the symptoms, and what a quick assessment reveals. For most standard blockages, a professional-grade mechanical snake or drain auger breaks through the clog and restores flow quickly.

If the blockage is more severe — grease accumulation, compacted solids, or a recurring problem — we recommend hydro jetting to clear the pipe completely and prevent rapid recurrence. For commercial properties, we can service multiple drain lines in a single visit. We confirm flow is fully restored before leaving, and if we discover a more serious underlying issue, we show you the evidence and walk through your options.

Signs You Need This Service

  • Water draining slowly from sinks, tubs, or shower floors
  • Gurgling or bubbling sounds when water drains
  • Foul odors coming from one or more drains
  • Water backing up into a tub or floor drain when you flush the toilet
  • Multiple drains throughout the property running slow at the same time
  • Drain has completely backed up and will not clear with a plunger

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you handle commercial floor drains and grease lines differently from residential drains?
Yes. Commercial drain cleaning involves higher-volume lines, FOG (fats, oils, and grease) accumulation, and often compliance requirements that residential work does not. Our team is experienced in both, and we bring the appropriate equipment for commercial-scale applications.
How do I know whether I need drain cleaning or a full sewer repair?
A camera inspection usually gives us the clear answer. If the drain is slow or backing up but the pipe itself is structurally intact, cleaning resolves it. If there is a crack, collapse, or severe root intrusion, repair may be needed. We use the camera to give you a direct, honest answer before recommending anything beyond cleaning.
Can you clean storm drains and area drains in addition to interior drains?
Yes. We service storm inlets, catch basins, area drains, and parking lot drains in addition to interior plumbing drains. If your property has a drainage system — commercial, industrial, or residential — we can service it.
