How Underground Pipe Services Works

Every underground pipe project begins with a camera inspection and, where necessary, underground utility locating to identify all subsurface utilities in the work zone. Once we have a complete picture of what is underground, we develop a scope of work and present you with all available method options along with honest pricing for each.

Trenchless methods — CIPP lining for pipe rehabilitation and pipe bursting for full replacement — minimize disruption to landscaping, driveways, and hardscaping. Open-cut excavation is used when trenchless methods are not feasible, when new lines need to be installed from scratch, or when depth and access require it. All underground work is performed to code, documented with location records, and verified with a final camera pass.

Signs You Need This Service

Frequently Asked Questions

What is pipe bursting and when is it used?
Pipe bursting is a trenchless replacement method where a conical bursting head is pulled through the existing pipe, fracturing it outward into the surrounding soil while simultaneously pulling a new pipe into place behind it. It is used when an existing pipe needs full replacement but a continuous excavation trench would be impractical or too disruptive to the surface above.
Do underground pipe projects require permits?
In most cases, yes — especially for sewer lateral work, water service replacements, and any work within the public right-of-way. We handle permit procurement and ensure all work passes inspection and is properly documented to code. We will tell you upfront which permits are required and include those costs in your estimate.
How do you handle landscaping and driveways during excavation?
We use equipment appropriate to the site, exercise care around tree root zones, and restore disturbed surfaces to as-close-to-original condition as the project scope allows. For driveways and hardscaping, we patch what we disturb. When trenchless is a viable option, we use it specifically to minimize that surface disruption. We are transparent about what restoration is included in the project scope.
