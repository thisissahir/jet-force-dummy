Grease Trap Cleaning in Denver, CO

Stay compliant, stay open. Scheduled grease trap pumping and cleaning for commercial kitchens of all sizes.

Every commercial kitchen that handles cooking produces FOG — fats, oils, and grease — and that material has to be captured before it enters the municipal sewer system. A properly functioning grease trap does exactly that. When the trap is not maintained, it overflows, backs up into the kitchen, creates serious odor problems, and puts you at risk of health code violations, municipal fines, and forced closure.

Jet Force provides scheduled grease trap cleaning for restaurants, cafeterias, institutional kitchens, food processing facilities, and any commercial kitchen in the Denver metro area. We pump, clean, inspect, and document every service — giving you the paper trail required for regulatory compliance and the peace of mind that comes from a properly functioning system.

How Grease Trap Cleaning Works

Our technician arrives at the scheduled time, removes the trap covers, and pumps out the full contents — including the floating FOG layer, the wastewater zone, and the settled solids at the bottom. The interior baffles, walls, and components are then scrubbed and cleaned to restore the trap to its full operational capacity.

After cleaning, we inspect the trap components for wear or damage and document the service in our compliance log. You receive a service record noting the date, technician, volume removed, and trap condition — documentation that is required by many local health departments and municipal FOG compliance programs. All waste is transported and disposed of properly per Colorado regulations.

Signs You Need This Service

  • Slow floor drains or sink drains in the kitchen area
  • Sewage or grease odors coming from drains or the trap itself
  • Grease or solids visible backing up into sinks or floor drains
  • Trap is approaching or has exceeded 25% capacity
  • More than 90 days have passed since the last service
  • A health department inspection or FOG compliance audit is upcoming

Frequently Asked Questions

How often does a grease trap need to be pumped?
Most health codes require service when the trap reaches 25% capacity — for many kitchens, that is every 30 to 90 days. High-volume restaurants may need monthly service. We assess your operation and recommend the right frequency rather than defaulting to a schedule that benefits us instead of you.
What happens if grease trap maintenance is neglected?
FOG overflows into the municipal sewer system, where it solidifies and causes major blockages. This can result in sewage backing up into your kitchen, significant fines from your municipality, failed health inspections, and in serious cases, a forced temporary closure. Regular maintenance costs a fraction of any of those consequences.
Do you provide compliance documentation after each service?
Yes. Every service includes a written record noting the date, technician, volume removed, and trap condition. This documentation is required by many local health authorities and municipal FOG compliance programs, and we ensure every record is accurate and easy to file with your health department if requested.
